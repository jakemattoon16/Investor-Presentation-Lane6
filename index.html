Investor Presentation
Building the operating system for revenue backends.
Lane6 — lane6.ai
The Problem
Mid-market firms run revenue operations on duct tape.
The CRM, the outreach tool, the onboarding workflow, and the pipeline tracker are four different products owned by four different vendors.
No one inside the firm operates the backend end to end. Leads fall through. Onboarding is manual. The system leaks.
Fragmented, manual, and fragile. That is the status quo for every firm that has not solved this.
The Solution
Lane6 builds and operates the revenue backend as infrastructure.
Recurring, sticky, and operator-owned.
Recurring
Infrastructure revenue model
Clients pay to have Lane6 operate their backend on an ongoing basis, not for a one-time build.
Sticky
Deep operational integration
Lane6 becomes the operator layer inside the client business. Switching means rebuilding the entire backend.
Operator-Owned
We run it, not just deliver it
No hand-off. Lane6 is accountable for the backend performing. That relationship compounds over time.
Why Now
The tooling consolidated. The operators did not.
GoHighLevel and platforms like it have made the infrastructure affordable and accessible. But operating them well is still the bottleneck.
The shift that happened
All-in-one revenue tooling is now accessible at a fraction of the cost of enterprise stacks.
GoHighLevel has become the infrastructure layer for mid-market revenue backends.
The tools are ready. The firms that know how to run them at a professional level are not.
The Gap
Lane6 is the operator layer
Most firms either use these tools badly or not at all. Lane6 is the team that builds and operates the backend professionally, the way a SaaS company would run its own stack. That gap is the business.

Competitive Landscape
Lane6 is the operator layer. Everything else is a tool.
The alternatives
Point solutions: CRM vendors, outreach tools, automation platforms. Clients buy the tool and figure out the rest themselves.
DIY tooling: Firms assemble their own stack. Nobody operates it professionally. It works until it does not.
Agencies: Build and hand off. Not operators. No ongoing accountability.
Most Direct Threat
Jump.ai
Jump.ai sells AI-powered revenue tooling for financial advisors. Strong product. But the model is tool-first: they sell the software and the client runs it. Lane6 is operator-first: we build the backend and run it. The differentiation is in who holds operational accountability over time. That is where Lane6 wins.
